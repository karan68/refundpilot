The Problem

Two Customers. Same Broken System.

Indian e-commerce treats every refund identically — a loyal customer waits just as long as a serial abuser. No intelligence. No autonomy. No differentiation.

👩

Priya's Problem

Ordered an ₹800 cotton kurta. It arrived with a tear on the sleeve. She raised a refund. Then she waited.

3 days for a reply. Support asked for photos via email. 5 more days. Finally approved. Then 7 more days for the money.

Total: 15 days. On Day 3, she left a 1-star review. She never shopped there again.

The merchant lost a lifetime customer over ₹800 — an amount they were always going to approve anyway.

Lost Customer. Lost Revenue.
🕵️

Rohit's Problem

Returned 8 of his last 12 orders. Always "damaged in transit." He signed for every delivery. Never provided photos when asked.

He cost one merchant ₹47,000 that year. Nobody noticed because every refund looked identical in a manual queue.

And he's filing the same claims at 2 OTHER Pine Labs merchants. Nobody connects the dots.

₹47K Fraud. Undetected.
The fundamental design flaw: the refund decision isn't a queue problem — it's an intelligence problem. What if an autonomous AI agent could look at the refund request, pull the customer's history, check for fraud patterns, and make a decision in 4 seconds?
The Solution

The 4-Second Autonomous Loop

RefundPilot is an autonomous AI agent. It observes, collects evidence, scores risk with deterministic math, decides using a 3-tier system, and acts — all without human involvement.

1

OBSERVE

Refund request arrives via API, chat, or WhatsApp (Hindi/English)

2

COLLECT

Agent pulls 10 signals: delivery status, history, RFM, cross-merchant intel

3

SCORE

Deterministic weighted formula computes risk (0–100). Same input = same output.

4

DECIDE

3-tier logic: math decides clear cases, AI judges gray areas

5

ACT

Pine Labs Refund API / Evidence request / Store credit / Case brief

"Instant" means instant DECISION, not instant money. RefundPilot makes the decision in ~4 seconds. The actual money flows after return logistics (or immediately for returnless items under ₹500).

3-Tier Decision Logic

Clear cases don't need expensive LLM reasoning — math is faster, cheaper, deterministic. The gray zone is where human-like judgment matters.

0–30

Auto-Approve

Math decides. No LLM needed. Instant, deterministic. LLM only generates explanation text. Return pickup auto-scheduled or returnless for items under ₹500.

Math Decides ⚡
31–70

Investigate (ReAct Loop)

AI decides. Agent enters a tool-use loop: request evidence, analyze photos, offer store credit, check cross-merchant data. Each step streamed live.

AI Decides 🔍
71–100

Escalate

Math decides. No debate. Human gets a pre-built case brief with top signals, counterfactual, and one-click action buttons. Zero repeated work.

Risk Intelligence

The 10-Signal Scoring Model

Inspired by Appriss Retail (370M+ returns/year) and Signifyd. Deterministic, auditable, transparent. Every signal's contribution is visible.

RiskScore = Σ (weighti × signal_scorei)    where Σ weights = 1.0
Same input → same output. Always. The LLM never touches the score — only explains it.
# Signal Weight What It Catches
S1 Customer Refund Rate 0.18 Chronic returners — most predictive single signal in retail fraud
S2 Delivery Contradiction 0.14 "Damaged" but signed for delivery — binary, high precision
S3 Amount Risk 0.07 High-value claims get more scrutiny
S4 Claim Pattern Repetition 0.11 Same excuse used 3+ times
S5 Product Category Deviation 0.07 Returns above category norm
S6 Sentiment Analysis 0.06 Formulaic/copy-paste messages → suspicious
S7 RFM: Recency 0.08 Recent refund = higher suspicion
S8 RFM: Frequency 0.08 Burst of refunds in 90 days
S9 RFM: Monetary Ratio 0.08 Refunded ₹ exceeds purchased ₹
S10 Cross-Merchant Fraud Pine Labs 0.12 Claims at OTHER Pine Labs merchants in 30 days — the X-factor
S10 is only possible because Pine Labs processes payments across thousands of merchants. The agent queries: "Has this customer filed refund claims at OTHER Pine Labs merchants in the last 30 days?" This is Signifyd's billion-dollar differentiator — built natively on Pine Labs for India.

Core Infrastructure

Pine Labs: Load-Bearing in Both Directions

Pine Labs isn't a payment gateway bolted on — it's the data backbone. The system literally cannot work without it.

📥 Data IN (Feeds Scoring)

  • S10 Cross-Merchant: Pine Labs network reveals claims at other merchants in 30 days
  • S9 Monetary Ratio: Transaction history provides total purchase amounts
  • Fraud Ring: Shared payment methods across Pine Labs merchants
  • S3 Amount: Original transaction amount from Pine Labs, not just order DB
←
PINE
LABS
→

📤 Data OUT (Executes Actions)

  • Refund API: POST /refunds/{order_id} — processes refund to original payment method
  • Payment Links: POST /paymentlink — real clickable links for store credit
  • Orders: POST /orders — creates tracking orders for every decision
  • Settlements: GET /settlements — reconciliation tracking (F24)
The chargeback angle: Every fraudulent refund RefundPilot catches is a chargeback Pine Labs doesn't have to process. Dispute fees (₹1,500–₹3,000 per case) + merchant account health + Pine Labs' own processing costs. This is Pine Labs' problem too, not just the merchant's.

Research Foundation

Standing on the Shoulders of Giants

RefundPilot's approach is grounded in industry practice and academic research — not invented in a vacuum.

1
Appriss Retail / The Retail Equation

370M+ returns/year processed for Home Depot, Best Buy, Victoria's Secret. Weighted behavioral scoring is their core methodology.

2
Signifyd ($1.7B valuation)

Commerce protection via merchant network intelligence across 10,000+ merchants. Same cross-merchant concept as our S10.

3
Riskified (RSKD, publicly traded)

ML-based e-commerce fraud prevention using behavioral signals + network effects.

4
RFM Analysis — Bult & Wansbeek (1995)

"Optimal Selection for Direct Mail." Marketing Science, 14(4). Foundation of Recency-Frequency-Monetary analysis in retail.

5
RFM Customer Segmentation — Fader et al. (2005)

"Counting Your Customers the Easy Way." Marketing Science, 24(2). Extended RFM to probabilistic customer lifetime models.

6
Pine Labs Plural API

Payment gateway APIs for orders, refunds, payment links, settlements. Real UAT integration in our demo.

7
ZhipuAI GLM-5

LLM for reasoning and explanation generation in the ReAct agentic loop.

8
Groq Llama 4 Scout

Fast inference for vision analysis — stock photo detection, damage assessment.
